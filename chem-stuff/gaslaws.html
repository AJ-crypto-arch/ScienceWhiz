Gas Laws

What Are the Different Gas Laws?

Boyle's Law: P1V1 = P2V2. When the volume of a gas increases, the pressure of the gas decreases and visa versa. Gay-Lussac's Law: (P1/T1) = (P2/T2). When the pressure of a gas decreases, the temperature decreases and visa versa. Charle's Law: (V1/T1) = (V2/T2). When the volume of a gas decreases, the temperature decreases and visa versa.

These laws help describe what happens to the gasses if one of the attributes changes.
