What is Zeff?

Zeff = Z - S (Z being number of protons and S being the number of inner electron) you can find number of inner electrons by using the atomic number of the noble gas that is before your atom. E.g, Phosphorus's number of inner electrons is 10 (Ne) The attraction the valence electrons have for the protons in the nucleus. This is in order to quantify the effect of the relative attraction onto the valence electrons from the nucleus.
The lower the Zeff, the bigger the radius as there is less attraction from the nucleus and the valence shell. More attraction means the valence electrons are closer to the nucleus which means a lower radius.

When calculating Zeff in the d or f blocks, you need to take into account that the electron in the d and f orbitals are inner electrons, not valence electrons. More attraction means the valence electrons are closer to the nucleus which means a lower radius of the atom.
