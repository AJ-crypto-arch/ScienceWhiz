Kinematics

Kinematics is the study of movement without considering the apsect of mass or the forces that cause them to move. Defined by 5 equations, kinematics include 5 main variables to solve or define. They are displacement(Δx), time(t), acceleration(a), Initial velocity(v0), and Final Velocity(vf). The equations are defined as follows:

$$v_{avg} = \frac{v_f+v_0}{2}$$ $$v_f = v_0 + at$$ $$\triangle x =v_0t + \frac{1}{2}at^2$$ $$v_f^2 = v_0^2 + 2a\triangle x$$ $$\triangle x = v_{avg}t = \frac{v_f+v_0}{2}t$$

Using these equations, we can solve for almost all problems involving movement without considering mass, including movement on inclines through trigonometric functions such as sin() and cos().
