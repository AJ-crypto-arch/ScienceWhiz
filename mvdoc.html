Momentum

Linear/Translational momentum is the product of the mass and velocity of an object, and is often represented by the equation $$p = mv.$$ Impulse is defined as the change in momentum, and is often denoted by Δp or I. The equations of them are $$Δp = mv_f - mv_i = m(v_f-v_i)$$ and $$I = Ft.$$

Often, you would see this as a composition of the x and y axes, and so we must use cos() and sin() to find the lengths of the momentum vectors. As seen below, it can be used for elastic and non-elastic collisions(Non-elastic is where the 2 objects colliding stick together to form a single object, while non-elastic collisions allow the objects to bounce of eachother, making velocity and direction change.)
